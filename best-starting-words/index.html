Where the Famous Starters Rank

SLATE — WordleBot's longtime favorite — comes in at #24 (1.70 letters, but an excellent 0.64 greens). CRANE, the pick made famous by 3Blue1Brown's information-theory video, ranks #46 (1.67 letters, 0.62 greens). Both remain great openers: they trade a little coverage for green-heavy positioning.

And the myth: ADIEU ranks #217, hitting just 1.51 letters per answer — and AUDIO is way down at #613 (1.34). Stacking vowels feels productive, but vowels are cheap information: E and A appear in nearly half of all answers anyway. It's the common consonants — R, T, L, S — that actually narrow the field.

How We Ranked These Words

Every candidate word was scored against the complete archive of real Wordle answers (1,826 answers through July 2026 — see the full list). For each answer we count how many of the starter's distinct letters appear in it (a green or yellow in an actual game) and how many sit in the exact right position (greens). Rankings use average letters hit, with greens as the tiebreaker. As new answers are played daily, the numbers drift very slowly; the top tier has been stable for years.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best Wordle starting word?

STARE is our pick for best all-round starter: 1.79 letters hit per real answer with an excellent 0.60 greens. ROATE and ORATE score marginally higher on coverage but are obscure words that have never been Wordle answers.

Is ADIEU a good starting word?

No — ADIEU ranks around #217 against real answers (1.51 letters hit). Common consonants like R, T, S and L narrow the field faster than a fourth vowel does.

What are the best two starting words to play together?

LATER → SONIC: 3.08 letters covered per answer on average, and 99.3% of all real Wordle answers contain at least one of their ten letters.

Do the best starting words change over time?

Slightly. These rankings are recomputed against the growing archive of real answers — one new word per day — but the percentages move very slowly and the top tier has been stable for years. Check whether any word has already been used on our past Wordle answers list.
