Finished today's puzzle in three and hungry for more? These are the alternatives that have earned a daily slot, grouped by what they change about the formula. All are playable free in a browser unless noted.

More Boards, Same Rules

Pure Wordle mechanics, multiplied. Every guess types onto all boards at once — the skill is triaging which board to serve next.

Quordle 4 boards · 9 guesses

The definitive board-multiplier, now run by Merriam-Webster. Four puzzles share your keyboard; nine guesses total. The opening two guesses become pure information plays — our starter rankings matter double here.

Dordle 2 boards · 7 guesses

The gentlest step up: two boards, seven guesses. The right on-ramp before attempting the bigger multipliers.

Octordle 8 boards · 13 guesses

Eight simultaneous boards with thirteen guesses. Brutal but fair — there's almost no slack for a wasted guess, so word-family traps (see habit 6) will end you.

Twists on the Grid

Same color language, different puzzle underneath.

Waffle rearrange · 15 swaps

All the letters are already on the board — a waffle-shaped grid of six crossing words — and you rearrange them in 15 swaps or fewer. More logic puzzle than vocabulary test; five-star solves feel earned.

Squaredle word search

Find every word hidden in a letter grid by tracing adjacent tiles. Less about guessing, more about exhaustive discovery; the daily grid has a satisfying "one more word" pull.

Crosswordle reverse Wordle

Works backwards: you're shown a finished Wordle grid's colors and must reconstruct guesses that produce exactly that pattern. Sharpens your understanding of how the coloring rules actually work — especially with repeated letters.

Absurdle adversarial

Wordle that cheats: there's no fixed answer, and the game actively shifts its secret word to give you the least helpful feedback possible. Unlimited guesses; beating it in four feels like outsmarting a chess engine.

Meaning Over Spelling

For players who want the word itself to matter more than its letters.

Semantle semantic similarity

Guess any word — Semantle scores how close you are in meaning, not spelling. Expect dozens of guesses and a genuinely different kind of thinking; "warm" here means conceptually adjacent, which is wonderfully disorienting.

The NYT Stable

If Wordle is your morning ritual, these live one tap away in the same app.

Connections group 16 words

Sixteen words, four hidden groups of four, four mistakes allowed. The purple category is reliably diabolical. Arguably the daily puzzle people now argue about most.

Strands theme word search

A themed word search with a hidden "spangram" tying the board together. Gentler than Connections, great for a second coffee.

Spelling Bee 7 letters · pangrams

Make as many words as possible from seven letters, always using the center letter. Chasing "Genius" rank is the closest thing puzzles have to a slow-burn addiction. Full access needs an NYT Games subscription.

Beyond Words Entirely

The Wordle feedback loop, exported to other domains.

Nerdle math equations

Guess the hidden eight-character equation instead of a word — digits and operators get the green/yellow/gray treatment. The commutative property is your best opening trick.

Worldle geography

Identify a country from its silhouette; wrong guesses return distance and direction arrows. Painlessly rebuilds the geography you forgot after school.

Globle geography · heat

Guess the mystery country with only hotter/colder color feedback on a 3D globe. Harder than Worldle — no silhouette to anchor on.

Frequently Asked Questions

What game is most similar to Wordle?

Dordle and Quordle — identical five-letter rules, just played on two or four boards at once with a shared keyboard.

Are Wordle alternatives free to play?

Most are free in the browser, including Quordle, Octordle, Waffle, Semantle, Nerdle and Worldle. NYT's Connections and Strands are free daily; Spelling Bee is partially behind the NYT Games subscription.

What is the hardest Wordle alternative?

Octordle for raw board count, Absurdle because it actively dodges your guesses, and Semantle because meaning-based feedback regularly takes dozens of guesses.
