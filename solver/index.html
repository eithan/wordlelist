A Wordle Helper With a Clue No Solver Has

Every Wordle solver can filter words by your greens, yellows and grays. This one knows something extra: whether today's answer has ever been played before. Most days it hasn't — which means every one of the 1,800+ past Wordle answers is impossible and gets crossed out automatically. On the rare repeat day, the logic flips: only past answers remain. Either way the candidate list shrinks dramatically, and it never uses the answer itself — just a yes/no fact.

Recommended guesses surface the most common English words still consistent with your board, so the picks are words you'll actually recognize rather than obscure letter-dumps. Frequently Asked Questions

How do I use this Wordle helper?

Tap the board and type a guess you've played, then tap each tile to cycle its color until it matches your real board. The recommendations update instantly as you type: greens and yellows apply the moment you set them, and gray letters count once their row is complete. When you finish a row, a new one appears carrying the clues you've already earned: green letters locked into place, and any letter you marked yellow held at least yellow. Use the × beside a row to clear it.

What makes this different from other solvers?

The played-before clue. We maintain the complete archive of every Wordle answer, so this helper can rule out words no other tool can — without spoiling anything.

Does it spoil today's answer?

No. It uses only a yes/no fact — whether today's word has been played before — never the answer itself. What you see is determined entirely by the guesses you enter.

How are repeated letters handled?

It simulates Wordle's exact coloring rules. A gray tile for a letter that's yellow or green elsewhere correctly means "no more copies of this letter" — the case most solvers get wrong.
